Dynamic Swarm Robotics for Sustaining Natural Environments

Decentralized Control · Triangular Lattice Formation · Obstacle Avoidance

This project develops a swarm capable of uniform coverage and structural stability for tasks such as environmental monitoring, cleanup, and conservation — operating safely in cluttered or hazardous terrains.

Goals Achieved

  • Maintain a triangular lattice for precise, uniform area coverage
  • Dynamically avoid obstacles without breaking formation
  • Converge reliably to target locations across varying environments

Control Strategy

Each robot uses a fully decentralized control strategy, computing its motion using four local interaction forces:

  • Cohesion — pulls toward the swarm centroid to maintain formation
  • Alignment — steers toward the target destination
  • Inter-robot Repulsion — prevents collisions within the swarm
  • Obstacle Avoidance — "virtual" obstacle agents enable robots to bypass barriers and seamlessly reform the lattice

Results & Performance

MATLAB-based simulations confirm that the triangular lattice persists even in cluttered environments. Performance metrics reveal:

  • Robust navigation efficiency across different obstacle densities
  • Consistently low computation latency per time step
  • Connectivity analysis confirms each robot remains connected to other swarm members at every moment
